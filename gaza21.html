Published July 30, 2025Updated Sept. 22, 2025
Leer en español

France announced its recognition of the state of Palestine at the United Nations General Assembly on Monday. Hours later, Luxembourg, Malta and Andorra followed. Australia, Britain, Canada and Portugal had confirmed their recognition on Sunday.

Before the General Assembly, most countries in the U.N. had already recognized a Palestinian state. Palestine is currently a nonmember observer state in the organization.

Belgium had also announced its intent to recognize Palestinian statehood. On Monday, its prime minister said his country was joining the movement, but that it would not “legally recognize” the state until Hamas released the Israeli hostages and is removed from Palestine.

The announcements were made in an effort to help salvage the prospect of a two-state solution to the Israeli-Palestinian conflict. They also reflect the deep frustrations with Israel’s conduct of the war in Gaza, which has killed tens of thousands of Palestinians and left a population of about two million in a state of extreme privation and hunger.

Several other European states, including Ireland, Norway and Spain, recognized a Palestinian state last year.
